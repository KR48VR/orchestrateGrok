Original Prompts Used in Examples

Query 1:
“Summarize All-In Podcast’s view on AI coding agents”
Query 2:
“Help me decide whether to invest in AI infrastructure companies — compare data center stocks, energy, and chips.”

Normal Mode

All-In Podcast is very bullish on AI coding agents. Recent episodes with Marc Benioff highlight how tools from Anthropic (Claude) are making coding dramatically cheaper and faster. Benioff says he's spending ~$300M this year on Anthropic and that agents let Salesforce do things they couldn't before — humans + agents working together.

They see agents disrupting SaaS (some companies will thrive by focusing on customers/innovation, others will die if they don't adapt). Jensen Huang clip: every engineer will soon have 100 agents, shifting work to creativity and orchestration rather than raw coding.

Overall tone: Massive efficiency gains, but it will reshape software companies and valuations.

Orchestrate Mode

Orchestrator Plan → Researcher + Analyst + Critic + Synthesizer

  • Enthusiasm Level: Extremely high. Benioff calls coding agents “awesome” and a “rocket ship.”
  • Core View: Agents make everything cheaper/faster → unprecedented efficiency in service, marketing, and distribution.
  • SaaS Impact: Brutal adaptation — customer-obsessed companies win, others die. Public markets will punish fantasy valuations.
  • Broader Shift: Engineers move from writing code to specifying architectures and managing agent teams.
  • Caveats: Power/compute constraints (Chamath), open-source agents as potential disruptors.

Synthesis: Transformative and inevitable. Short-term disruption, long-term software productivity explosion and abundance.

Normal Mode

AI infrastructure is booming due to hyperscaler CapEx (hundreds of billions in 2026).

  • Chips: Nvidia (NVDA) dominant but high valuations.
  • Data Centers: EQIX, DLR, Vertiv (VRT) benefiting from rack density.
  • Energy: Constellation (CEG), Bloom Energy (BE) seeing huge demand from AI power needs.

Quick Take: Yes, invest for long-term tailwinds, but diversify. Chips are more mature; data centers + energy have more runway. Watch Big Tech CapEx updates. Risks: interest rates, overbuild.
